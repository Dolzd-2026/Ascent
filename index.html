The faith-based advising framework

When advising a Muslim business leader — four lenses:

Niyyah — What is the intention? Is this for Allah's pleasure or for ego?
Shura — Who is being consulted? Are they surrounded by honest counsel?
Adl — Is this fair? To employees, community, environment?
Tawakkul — After analysis, can they act decisively and trust Allah with the outcome?

You fell off.
That's fine. You're back now.

High performers fall off. It's not a character flaw — it's the cost of running at maximum capacity for weeks. The system was designed knowing this would happen. Here's the protocol.

🔄 The 5-step re-entry protocol

Do these in order. Takes less than 5 minutes.

1
Do not catch up. Whatever you missed is gone. Start from this week. Catching up is the #1 reason people stay off track.
2
Switch to Bare Minimum Mode for your first week back. One ACCA chapter. One podcast. Three sentences. Nothing else.
3
Don't tell yourself a story. "I always fall off." No. You had a brutal stretch. Now you're back. That's the whole story.
4
Text your husband one sentence: "Starting again this Saturday." You just said it out loud. That's your commitment device.
5
Tap the Today tab. Check one box. The streak restarts at one. That's fine. You're back.
Why you fell off — and what to do differently
Trigger: Brutal work week wiped me out
→ Switch to Bare Minimum immediately. Don't wait until you have energy for Full Mode. The habit survives on 20 minutes.
Trigger: Novelty wore off, feels like a chore
→ Change the content, not the system. Swap the podcast. Try a new Islamic source. Pick a different sector topic. The structure stays — the content rotates.
Trigger: Lost track, didn't know how to re-enter
→ You're on this tab right now. That's re-entry. Go to Today, switch to Bare Minimum, check one box.
Trigger: Struggling with routines generally
→ Don't build routines around time. Build them around triggers: coffee = ACCA. Phone face-down at night = podcast. These don't require remembering — they happen automatically.

Islamic investing principles you are applying
☪️ Riba-free: No interest-bearing accounts, conventional bonds, or fixed-return instruments. Seek sukuk, mudarabah equity structures.
☪️ Halal screening: Avoid companies with >5% revenue from haram activities (alcohol, tobacco, gambling, conventional banking). IFG screener automates this.
